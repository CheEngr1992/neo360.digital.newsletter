Hawaii Luxury Living Journey

The Honolulu Board of Realtors reports that Single Family Home average sales are up a little over 15 (15.5)percent since last year at the same time. Condo sales prices were are up a little over thirty-seven (37.8) compared to their report however those results can be a little decieving since we should consider the trend from this current year for the past three (3) months instead. If you actually compare the past few months, the average price suggests the increase is more along the lines of three (3) percent from February's Average Single Family price of $1,363,998 to $1,403,055 in April. The clear differiator is that last year around this time the higher interest rates were still being normalized by the market. According to various economic predictions, the Federal Reserve Bank is slated to conduct two rate cuts this year but given recent inflation numbers, that may be wishful thinking for this year. Some anlaysts think that there may not be any rate cuts this year due to all the numbers

Developers such as Howard Hughes, Kobayashi Group, and Avalon Group LLC continue to sell and open up new condominium units in Kakaako and Downtown. As older buildings in Honolulu experience higher assesments due to Fire and Safety requirements as well as Hurricane Insurance changes, these newer buildings may be more attractive for buyers who want a newer building that are already equipped with upto date requirements. Overall there is a lack of inventory in Honolulu for Single Family Homes and given the Governor's SB2919 to allow the County to regulate the time, place, manner, and Duration in which uses of land and structure may take place, we anticipate changes in the market continues to shift.
